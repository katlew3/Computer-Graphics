Remarks

  • Keep in mind that the OpenGL window's origin (0,0) is on the bottom-left, whereas mouse reports with (0,0) on top-left
  • In order to get the mouse-to-world coordinate mapping you can:
    • METHOD 1
      • Switch to orthogonal projection: glm::mat4 ProjectionMatrix = glm::ortho(-4.0f, 4.0f, -3.0f, 3.0f, 0.0f, 100.0f);
      • After you have gotten your CursorPos use the glm::unProject(glm::vec3(x, y, 0.0), ModelMatrix, ProjectionMatrix, Viewport); function to get your world coordinates
    • METHOD 2
      • Get the corresponding mouse coordinates for our vertices 0, 1, 2 by clicking on them and recording their mouse positions - glfwGetCursorPos(window, &xpos, &ypos);
      • Since you know the exact positions of vertices you can then figure out the mapping.
      e.g. Consider a vertex with world coordinates (1,1). Clicking on it and extracting its corresponding xpos and ypos would give you the mapping (1,1) -> (xpos,ypos).
      After extracting the mapping for the other 2 vertices you would be able to construct your reference frame.
      This needs to be done once and the mapping can be hardcoded.
